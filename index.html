About Me

I am a student of complexity — equally at home in the formal elegance of a mathematical proof, the structural logic of an economic model, and the intuitive craft of visual design.

Pursuing an Integrated MSc in Software Development at Gauhati University while concurrently reading Economics at IGNOU, I am building a foundation spanning theoretical computer science, formal methods, and quantitative reasoning. My long-term goal is to work at the frontier of theoretical computer science — computability, type theory, complexity, and the deep mathematical structures beneath computation.

I have a genuine fondness for functional programming — Haskell in particular — because it forces disciplined abstraction that I find intellectually honest. A background in Fine Arts informs everything I design.
