About Me

I am a student of complexity — equally at home in the formal elegance of a mathematical proof, the structural logic of an economic model, and the intuitive craft of visual design.

Currently pursuing an Integrated MSc in Software Development at Gauhati University while concurrently reading Economics at IGNOU, I am building a foundation that spans theoretical computer science, formal methods, and quantitative reasoning. My long-term goal is to work at the frontier of theoretical computer science — computability, type theory, complexity, and the deep mathematical structures underneath computation.

I have a genuine fondness for functional programming — Haskell in particular — because it forces a kind of disciplined abstraction that I find intellectually honest. I write code the way I want to write mathematics: with precision and without ambiguity.

My interests are deliberately pluralist. I read literature and poetry (Assamese verse among them), work through discrete mathematics with rigor, appreciate the formal grammar of economic theory, and find deep satisfaction in the places where these disciplines unexpectedly converge.

A background in Fine Arts informs everything I design. I approach interfaces the way I approach a composition — with attention to negative space, rhythm, and the user's perceptual journey through information.
